Brief History

Opened in 1937, the Golden Gate Bridge connects the city of San Francisco to Marin County, CA. Construction began during the Great Depression, and many people were given the opportunity to work and have a steady income. Lead by Joseph Strauss, Charles Ellis and Leon Moisseiff, they believed that they would be able to complete the billion dollar project for just $25-30 million dollars.
